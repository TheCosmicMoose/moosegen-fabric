# MooseGen

## General Features:
- Customized biome colors for every biome including sky, fog, water, and foliage colors.
- Smoother surface generation because I have disabled or significantly reduced surface cave entrances. This does make caving slightly harder, but I think I have struck a balance of smoothing out the surface and still making resources relatively accessible.
- Stony Cliffs: Steep areas are converted to stone rather than having massive brown hills of dirt.
- Sniffer loot table expansion: In addition to pitcher pods and torchflowers sniffers can dig up lush cave foliage and golden dandelions.
- Mountain biomes have a chance of naturally spawning Breezes, watch your step!
- Completely compatible with tools like Chunkbase, all changes are to Vanilla biomes, no new biomes are added, and structure placement should be unaffected.

## Overhauled Biomes
- Dark Forest: Overhauled to take inspiration from Northern California's Redwood Forest, also added a rare chance for a wild sniffer to spawn!
- Pale Garden: Remade to look like the aftermath of a forest fire, with an extra high density of of creaking, bringing even more danger to the night.
- Forest: Added old oak trees with a similar shape to vanilla dark oaks, made the trees in general taller to make walking through them much easier. I also added patches of podzol ground cover as well as game trails of coarse dirt running throughout the biomes.
- Birch Forest: Added patches of moss around trees and made them much taller to better resemble real birch and aspen forests.
- Desert: Completely overhauled the ground cover to be made up of coarse dirt, packed mud, and red sand with sparse mesquite bushes and palo alto trees to resemble the Sonoran and Chihuauan deserts.
- Badlands: Now the primary source of regular sand, the Badlands have been overhauled to evoke images of Badlands National Park in North Dakota, with grassy, sandy foothills moving into terracotta bands, with wooded badlands populated with spruce and pine trees on the tops of plateaus.
- Taiga variants, brought podzol into the standard taiga, distinguished spruce and pine trees by having pines generate with stripped spruce wood. These also include the game trails of the standard forest.
- Swamp: added heavy muddy ground cover with patches of grass. Overhauled swamp oak trees to resemble willow trees.
- Beach: Added palm trees made of jungle wood and acacia leaves. Added tiedpools that can spawn coral fans and sea cucumbers.
- Stony Shore: Added stony tidepools with sparse seagrass.
- Snowy Beach: changed out the sandy ground cover for gravel, and added tidepools with dead coral fans and sea grass.
- River: Rivers now generate with sandy banks, clay beds, and where they are steep on the sides stone. Rivers can also rarely spawn sea cucumbers and have occasional lily pads along them.

## Planned Features and Addons
- Improvements to ocean biomes
- Modernized Village designs with materials changed to match biome asthetics.
- Custom Sapling Behaviors: Add the ability to grow saplings into different trees under different circumstances, e.g. placing 4 dark oak saplings on podzol or moss will generate a redwood while placing them on grass will generate a normal dark oak tree to preserve farm compatibility.
- Adding support for all new vanilla biomes as they come out, and backporting new features where they fit better than current implementations. E.g. adding shelf mushrooms to existing forest biomes with the 26.3 update etc.
- Renaming significantly overhauled biomes so they can be better found through the /locate command. This will not affect Chunkbase compatibility as I do not plan to change their placements, just their names.
- Extending biomes into caves: e.g making desert caves out of sandstone, and adding hanging roots to forest caves.
- Nether and End Biome Updates.
- Cave Re-Enabler: Creating an addon datapack that returns vanilla cave generation.

## Compatibility
- Likely not compatible with other worldgen mods (have not tested mods that just add new biomes, these may work) but almost certainly will break with Terralith, Tectonic, etc.
